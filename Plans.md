PeaPod Full Product Report

1. Product Overview

PeaPod is a smart social music platform that learns how people actually listen to music, then uses that data to create better solo playlists, better group playlists, and better shared music experiences.

Most music apps recommend songs based on broad taste. PeaPod goes further by learning patterns:
	•	what you listen to at certain times
	•	what music you play in certain moods
	•	what songs you post
	•	what songs you repeat
	•	what songs you skip
	•	how your taste changes depending on whether you are alone, in the car, working out, sad, locked in, or at a party

PeaPod is not just a music player. It is a music intelligence system.

2. Core Vision

PeaPod should feel like this:
	•	it understands your version of a mood
	•	it understands group compatibility
	•	it solves the “who gets the aux?” problem

	•	it gives users a feeling of identity, memory, and connection through music

Other apps recommend music you may like.

3. Main Product Pillars

PeaPod has four major pillars:

Pillar 1: Personal Music Intelligence

This is the foundation.

The app learns:
	•	your time-based music habits
	•	your repeated songs
	•	your favorite artists
	•	your mood-associated songs
	•	what you post versus what you casually play
	•	your phases over time

Examples:
	•	you play melodic rap most around 11 PM
	•	you post nostalgic songs after stressful days
	•	you prefer hype music on Fridays
	•	your gym music is different from your sad music
	•	your late-night drive music is different from your study music

This creates a personal Music Identity Profile.

⸻

Pillar 2: Group Sync Mode

This is your car mode / friend mode idea.

A few friends join a PeaPod session and the app builds a merged live playlist that balances:
	•	shared taste
	•	fairness
	•	context
	•	energy level
	•	predicted compatibility

Session controls include:
	•	Equal Play
	•	Lean Toward Driver
	•	Party Mode (Most Hype Wins)

This is stronger than a normal blend playlist because it is not just merging favorites. It is merging:
	•	listening behavior
	•	time patterns
	•	mood patterns
	•	likely compatibility

⸻

Pillar 3: Mood Chatbot

This is the conversational layer.

Users can type things like:
	•	I’m sad
	•	I need gym music
	•	I want late night drive songs
	•	I need to lock in
	•	give me something like what I listened to last month
	•	make me something that feels nostalgic

Instead of giving generic sad music, the chatbot checks what that mood means for that user specifically.

If your version of sad music is:
	•	Drake
	•	Brent Faiyaz
	•	slow melodic rap
then that is what the app should prioritize.

It can also reflect patterns back to the user:
	•	you’ve been listening to this vibe a lot this week
	•	want to stay in that mood or shift out of it?
	•	you usually play slower music around this time

This makes the system feel much smarter and more personal.

⸻

Pillar 4: Party Mode

This is the live social DJ system.

In a session:
	•	everyone can suggest songs
	•	everyone can vote on songs
	•	highest-voted songs move up in the queue
	•	the queue updates live
	•	PeaPod can fill in missing songs with AI suggestions when the crowd is quiet

This solves a very real problem:
who controls the music at parties, kickbacks, pregames, dorms, and hangouts?

Instead of passing the aux, PeaPod turns the room into a smart music democracy.

9. Step-by-Step Build Plan

Now the big part: how to build this in stages.

⸻

Phase 1: Define the MVP Foundation

Goal

Build the first version of PeaPod as a personal music pattern app.

What to include
	•	Spotify login
	•	user profile setup
	•	pull recent tracks
	•	pull top tracks
	•	pull top artists
	•	basic dashboard
	•	time-based pattern tracking
	•	first recommendation engine

Deliverables
	•	user can connect account
	•	user can see music history trends
	•	user can get “play 5 songs for me right now” suggestions

Why this first

Everything else depends on user-level pattern intelligence.

⸻

Phase 2: Build the Personal Music Identity Engine

Goal

Convert raw listening history into usable patterns.

Data to store
	•	song ID
	•	artist ID
	•	timestamp
	•	duration if available
	•	skip behavior if available
	•	repeat count
	•	user save/like status
	•	playlist source
	•	session type if known

Analytics to build
	•	favorite artists by time block
	•	favorite songs by time block
	•	genre by time block
	•	weekday/weekend preference
	•	repeated songs
	•	mood or energy clusters
	•	current trend versus historical trend

Output examples
	•	Your late-night taste leans melodic and slow
	•	Your Friday music is more upbeat than your Tuesday music
	•	You are currently in a reflective R&B phase

⸻

Phase 3: Build Smart Solo Playlist Generation

Goal

Turn pattern data into usable playlists.

Features
	•	playlist for right now
	•	playlist for current mood
	•	playlist based on time of day
	•	playlist based on historical patterns
	•	“something familiar”
	•	“something new but close”
	•	“something nostalgic”

Logic

Mix:
	•	strong known favorites
	•	pattern-consistent tracks
	•	some exploration
	•	some resurfaced forgotten songs

Output

A solo recommendation system that feels smarter than normal shuffle.

⸻

Phase 4: Add the Mood Chatbot

Goal

Give users a conversational way to ask for music.

MVP chatbot inputs
	•	I’m sad
	•	I want hype music
	•	give me study songs
	•	I need a late-night drive vibe
	•	play songs like the stuff I listened to last fall
	•	give me something smooth

MVP chatbot behavior
	•	detect mood or context
	•	map it to historical listening clusters
	•	generate playlist
	•	explain why it picked it

Example response

“You usually lean toward slower melodic rap when you feel like this. I built this from the songs you replay most late at night and a few similar tracks you haven’t played much yet.”

Later upgrades
	•	more open-ended chat
	•	emotional reflection
	•	better memory
	•	smarter phrase understanding

⸻

Phase 5: Build Group Sync Mode

Goal

Let multiple friends merge their taste intelligently.

Session flow
	•	host creates session
	•	friends join by code or QR
	•	each user authorizes music data
	•	app builds combined model
	•	app generates a shared queue

Required settings
	•	Equal Play
	•	Lean Toward Driver
	•	Party Mode (Most Hype Wins)

Equal Play logic

Everyone’s taste is weighted similarly.

Lean Toward Driver logic

Driver’s music taste has the strongest weight, but others still influence the queue.

Party Mode logic

Songs with higher collective hype/energy scores get prioritized.

Extra fairness logic
	•	no one user dominates too much
	•	shared songs rise first
	•	conflicts are softened by neutral middle-ground songs

⸻

Goal

Make groups feel naturally well-balanced.

Logic additions
	•	identify overlap artists
	•	identify overlap genres
	•	identify overlap energy bands
	•	identify disliked extremes
	•	rotate whose taste gets represented
	•	predict new songs the whole group might like

Output examples
	•	a queue with common favorites
	•	songs that bridge two different tastes
	•	AI-injected tracks likely to match everyone

Why this matters

This is how PeaPod becomes better than just throwing 3 playlists together.

Smart behavior

If the party is on a run of high-energy songs, PeaPod should not randomly inject something slow unless the room shows signs of wanting that shift.
